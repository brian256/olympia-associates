Hello — welcome to OpenPath.

We're building a way to turn volunteer and entry-level experience into a track record people can trust.

Why we're asking: before we build too much, we want to hear from people like you — whether you're looking for opportunities or trying to fill them. The questionnaire below is short, anonymous, and takes about five minutes. Nothing is tied to your name unless you choose to leave an email at the very end.

Why we're building this

Our hypothesis

Two problems, one shared root cause — and an idea for closing the gap between them.

For people

Your work isn't showing up on paper

Newcomers, career changers, and students earning work-experience credit have real skills — but no simple, trusted place to show them.

  • No "Canadian experience" to point to
  • References and volunteer hours scattered everywhere
  • Hard to tell which postings are legitimate
For organizations

Screening eats the time you don't have

Nonprofits, small businesses, and civic programs need reliable people for entry-level and volunteer roles — and lose hours filtering applicants who aren't a fit.

  • Large, undifferentiated applicant pools
  • No-shows and unclear reliability signals
  • Spreadsheets and email standing in for a real process
Our idea

One shared, trusted place

OpenPath would let people build a verified record of their volunteer and entry-level experience, and let organizations find and trust the right people faster — starting here in Edmonton.

  • Still just an idea — nothing is built yet
  • Your answers below decide what we build first

Does either of those feel familiar?

If you've felt either side of this — looking for opportunities, or trying to fill them — we'd genuinely like to hear from you. Anonymous, about five minutes, and it directly shapes what gets built first.
