Self-assessment

Is this service for you?

Read through these statements honestly. Each one describes a situation that is easy to overlook and expensive to discover late.

I've been sending applications and not hearing back — or getting interviews but not converting them to offers.

My résumé was built for a different market — it reads like it was written for employers in my home country, not in Canada.

My LinkedIn profile hasn't been updated in over a year and doesn't reflect where I'm going — only where I've been.

I have strong international credentials, but I feel like Canadian employers are discounting them without really seeing what I've done.

I'm changing industries or roles and I don't know how to position my existing experience for a target I haven't held before.

I've been told my experience is strong — but nobody has helped me translate it into language the right employers will immediately recognise.

I don't have a targeted employer list — I'm applying broadly and hoping something lands, rather than running a real campaign.

I'm a newcomer professional who has been in Canada less than two years and I'm still learning how this job market works.

I've had the same job search routine for months — and the results haven't changed.

I want to invest in a structured, professional approach — not keep doing what isn't working and expecting a different result.

If you said yes to six or more — your job search needs a professional rebuild, not more applications. Every month spent with the wrong tools is a month of opportunities that went to someone whose résumé told the story better than yours. A scoping call is free. Rebuilding your approach after another six months of silence is not.

How the engagement is structured

There are two zones. You start in one and transition to the other.

Job searches don't always resolve on a schedule. But a consulting engagement cannot run indefinitely — for your sake as much as ours. This is how we handle that honestly, without abandoning clients who haven't landed yet.

Zone 1 — Active Engagement

The contracted work. Defined scope. Full attention.

This is the paid engagement — the résumé, the LinkedIn profile, the campaign, the coaching. It runs for the defined scope and timeline in your contract. During this phase, you have direct access to Brian Ssennoga, active campaign support, and real-time adaptation as the search develops.

The active engagement ends when the scope is complete — not when the job is found. Most clients land during or shortly after this phase. Some do not — and that is handled honestly.

  • Full professional asset build
  • Active employer targeting and application tailoring
  • Direct consultant access throughout
  • Coaching calls and interview preparation included
Zone 2 — Olympia Community

Post-engagement support. Light-touch. Ongoing.

When the active engagement concludes, clients who haven't yet landed transition into the Olympia Community — a WhatsApp-based professional group where job opportunities, resources, and market intelligence are shared on an ongoing basis.

This is not a paid service. It is a community of professionals who have gone through the same process. Job leads get shared. Referrals happen. The relationship continues — just in a different mode.

  • WhatsApp-based — low friction, high relevance
  • Curated job opportunities shared as they surface
  • Peer connections with professionals in similar transitions
  • Occasional group resources and workshops
  • No time limit — stay as long as it's useful

Why this structure exists: rather than extending the engagement indefinitely at full consulting rate — which creates a bad dynamic for both parties — the Community Zone keeps the relationship alive and useful without misrepresenting what a consulting engagement can promise. We cannot guarantee a job. We can guarantee the tools, the campaign, and continued connection to opportunities after the contracted work is done.

Our model

One model. Built for the complexity of a real job search.

Done With You

We build the strategy together and run the campaign collaboratively.

We map your career preferences first — sector, org size, location, salary floor, role type. Then we build the assets together, target the employers, tailor each application, and adapt in real time. You're in it, not waiting on it. This model works across every client situation: a new market, a career change, internationally trained credentials, or a straightforward pivot. The accompaniment model means the thinking happens with you — not for you.

  • 1

    Scoping call — free, 30 min

    We discuss your situation, your target, and what a campaign for you would look like. Honest recommendation at the end.

  • 2

    Intake session — 1 hr

    Full work history, career preferences, target roles, and key achievements. Everything gathered in one focused session. This shapes every decision that follows.

  • 3

    Build phase

    Master résumé and LinkedIn built together, with rationale at each step. Multiple versions for different role types if needed. One revision round included.

  • 4

    Active campaign

    Target employer identification, application tailoring, post-interview follow-up. Run with you — not handed to you.

  • 5

    Handoff or transition

    If the role hasn't landed by engagement end, you move into the Olympia Community — an ongoing resource network for professionals still in search.
